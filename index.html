Anita Borg

January 17, 1949 - April 6, 2003 (aged 54)

    Time line
  • Jan 17 1949, born in Chicago
  • got her first programming job in 1969
  • In 1986, she began working for Digital Equipment Corporation, where she spent 12 years
  • In 1987, Borg founded Systers,[1] the first email network for women in technology.
  • In 1994, Anita Borg and Telle Whitney founded the Grace Hopper Celebration of Women in Computing.
  • received the Augusta Ada Lovelace Award from the Association for Women in Computing for her work on behalf of women in the computing field in 1995.
  • inducted as a Fellow of the Association for Computing Machinery in 1996.
  • In 1997, Borg left Digital Equipment Corporation and began working as a researcher in the Office of the Chief Technology Officer at Xerox PARC.
  • In 1999, President Bill Clinton appointed her to the Presidential Commission on the Advancement of Women and Minorities in Science, Engineering, and Technology. She was charged with recommending strategies to the nation for increasing the breadth of participation fields for women.
  • also in 1999, Borg was diagnosed with a brain tumor.
  • In 2002, she was awarded the 8th Annual Heinz Award for Technology, the Economy and Employment.[13] Also in 2002, Borg received an Honorary Doctor of Science and Technology degree from Carnegie Mellon University.
  • Apr 6 2003, died because of brain tumor.

Contributions
She founded the Institute for Women and Technology and the Grace Hopper Celebration of Women in Computing.
